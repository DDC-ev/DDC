The Great Re-alignment:
How central banks are quietly hedging against the dollar.

Julian Thorne

Chief Economics Editor

October 24, 2024 • 12 Minute Read

For nearly eight decades, the United States dollar has operated as the unchallenged sun around which the planetary economy orbits. But in the hushed corridors of Basel and Singapore, a new gravity is beginning to take hold.

The data is unmistakable, yet largely absent from the mainstream cycle. Central banks in the global south have increased their non-dollar reserves by nearly 14% in the last fiscal year alone. This isn't a loud exodus, but a calculated diversification—a hedging of bets in an increasingly bipolar world.

The Silent Shift

From the introduction of digital sovereigns to the resurgence of physical bullion as a strategic asset, the tools of fiscal independence are evolving. What remains to be seen is how the legacy institutions of the West will adapt to a world that no longer requires their permission to transact.
